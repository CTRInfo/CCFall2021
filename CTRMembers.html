Member Spotlight

Ololade A. Okito, MD, FAAP
  • Tell us a little bit about your family: 
    I am a first-generation Nigerian-American. My husband, Tim, is from Congo (DRC) and grew up in Ivory Coast and Utah. We live in Maryland with our 3 girls, ages 6- and 3- years and 10 months old. Tim is outnumbered and is learning to love all things pink and glittered. 
  • What is the one thing you cannot resist?
    Thai food! Love it!! I cannot wait to one day travel to Thailand and eat authentic Thai food all day long. 
  • Tell us something about yourself that would surprise us.
    I am addicted to motivational quotes. It is common for me to stop an exercise ride on my Peloton to write down a motivational quote from one of the instructors. One of my favorites is "if you're gonna breakdown, make sure you also breakthrough." Love it!! 

Efren Cazares, MPH

Efren Cazares is the new Research Business Manager overseeing the Center for Translational Research. Efren has been with Children’s National for just over 3 years. He has  a Master of Public Health from George Washington University and a Bachelor’s in Biochemistry from Willamette University.

Efren is originally from California and  currently resides in Maryland with his two dogs, a Samoyed named Oso and a French Bulldog named Haku. You will find Efren walking with Oso, renovating his house, or riding his motorcycle in his free time.

  • Favorite Travel Spot: 
    Japan. Every few years I make an attempt to go back to Japan. I hosted exchange students for a few semesters throughout college and managed to minor in Japanese Language. I'm a little rusty these days but I still manage to navigate, and importantly eat, my way through Japan every couple years. 
  • Tell us something about yourself that would surprise us.
    I grew up ~26 miles off the coast of Los Angeles on Catalina Island.
  • You’re Happiest When?
    I'm on the water. Having grown up on an island it was only natural that I would gravitate towards water sports - snorkeling, fishing, kayaking. I absolutely love it all. I even worked as an ocean kayak tour guide through most of high school. I spent most of my summers on ocean kayaks retelling stories of Catalina’s history and various endemic species. These days, I do most of my ocean kayaking off of Vinalhaven, ME. 
  • What is the one thing, you can’t live without?
    Oso. I mean look at him! He's adorable.
